Balsa Wood Pyramid

Jan. 2014 - Mar. 2014

This project was completed in a team of five for the American Society of Civil Engineer's Southeastern Conference held in March of 2014 in Tampa, Florida. The objective was to design and build a balsa wood pyramid capabale of withstanding an axial load while also maintaining as low a weight as possible under given dimensional constraints. Scoring was based on the stregnth to weight ratio. Overall, our pyramid withstood about 800 lbs during hydraulic loading. To achieve this, we used two types of balsa, differentiated by density. We had contest grade at 6.05 lb/ft3 and medium grade at 14.04 lb/ft3. Contest grade was typically used for the vertical members (desired flexibilty) and medium grade was typically used for horizontal and support members (desired rigidity). Placed 2nd overall.
